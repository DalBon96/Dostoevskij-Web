The Karamazov brothers

Published in 1880

The story revolves around Fyodor Karamazov, a corrupt and immoral father,
and his three sons: Dmitri (Mitya), the passionate eldest;
Ivan, the intellectual skeptic; and Alyosha, the deeply spiritual youngest.
When Fyodor is murdered, suspicion falls on Dmitri,
who had been in conflict with their father over money and a woman, Grushenka.
The novel explores the brothers' individual struggles with faith, free will,
and morality, while also addressing broader themes of justice, guilt, and the existence of God.
